Mouse Genome Sequencing Consortium

The mouse genome project is a broad international collaboration, including a genome-wide sequencing programme, targetted sequencing programmes; cDNA sequencing programmes and informatics programmes. The Mouse Genome Sequeuncing Consortium (MGSC) is a collaboration of four centres, working on a genome-wide basis to generate a finished sequence of the mouse genome, dovetailing with a number of targetted sequencing programmes.

  • The Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute - Mouse Sequencing Site
  • The Whitehead Institute/MIT Center for Genome Research
  • The Washington University Genome Sequencing Center
  • Ensembl, a joint project between the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute and EMBL-EBI

Physical Map of the Mouse Genome

A physical map of the mouse genome has been built using resources developed by the following centres:

  • Osoegawa and de Jong (CHORI) - Mouse BAC libraries
  • The Genome Sequence Centre - BAC fingerprint map of the mouse genome
  • The Institute of Genome Research - BAC end sequences

Targetting Mouse Sequencing Programmes

Incorporated into the final sequence of the mouse genome will be the sequence of regions targetted by specific projects. These include:

  • Trans-NIH BAC sequencing programme
    This funds sequencing of specific BACs/regions of biological interest by sequencing facilities at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, University of Oklahoma and the Harvard Partners Center for Genetics and Genomics.
  • UK - MRC Mouse Sequencing Programme
  • Joint Genome Institute
    Comparative sequencing of mouse chromosomal regions syntenic with human chromosome 19

cDNA Sequencing Programmes

  • The Riken Institute
  • The Mammalian Gene Collection
